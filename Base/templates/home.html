About Me

Who I am and what I do

I'm a passionate Computer Science student specializing in systems programming and hardware development. My journey in technology began when I built my first Arduino project, sparking a lifelong fascination with how software interacts with hardware at the most fundamental levels.

What drives me is solving complex problems where performance matters most. Whether it's optimizing memory usage in embedded systems or designing efficient algorithms, I thrive on challenges that require both technical depth and creative thinking.

When I'm not coding, you'll find me tinkering with hardware components, contributing to open-source projects, or exploring new technologies in the embedded systems space. I believe in writing clean, maintainable code that stands the test of time.
